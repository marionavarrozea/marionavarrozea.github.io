Characterization of Electric Propulsion Systems for VTOL RPAS

Research Framework: ProVANT-EMERGENTIa | ETSI Wind Tunnel, Seville

Python (Pandas, Scikit-Learn) Wind Tunnel Testing Data Analytics BLDC Propulsion

1. Project Context & Objectives

This study is part of the ProVANT-EMERGENTIa project, which focuses on the design of a fixed-wing convertible RPAS with VTOL capabilities for search-and-rescue (SAR) missions. Most wind tunnel experiments in the literature focus solely on propeller characterization, often neglecting the crucial influence of the motor. This research bridges that gap by providing an efficient testing methodology for the complete integrated motor-propeller system.

2. Hardware & Experimental Setup

  • Test Stand: Employed the Tyto Robotics Series 1585 Test Stand, capable of precisely measuring thrust, torque, voltage, and current simultaneously.
  • Motors & Propellers: Characterized five BLDC motors from Model Motors (AXI 2826/10 and AXI 4130/16, V1 and V2) and Turnigy (Aerodrive SK3 5045). These were paired with APC Thin Electric propellers (13x4E, 13x10E, 14x10E) to analyze the influence of pitch and diameter variations.
  • Power Supply: Integrated a SkyRC eFuel 1200W DC power supply, completely eliminating the dependence on charge cycles of traditional LiPo batteries and allowing for higher power tests.

3. Methodology & Data Pipeline

To produce accurate and reliable results, a robust data processing method was developed using Python:

  • Data Cleaning: Used the pandas library for data loading and the scikit-learn EllipticEnvelope function to automatically detect and remove the 10% of data points furthest from the statistical center.
  • Experimental Protocol: After comparing continuous and discrete sweeps, the discrete sweep method combined with constant speed experiments was selected to ensure the system reached mechanical equilibrium.

4. Results & Performance Analysis

  • Hardware Upgrades: Verified a significant improvement in the efficiency of the AXI V2 motors (up to 2.72%) compared to the V1 versions, attributed to enhanced cooling and reduced internal resistance.
  • Operational Regimes: Accurately mapped the thrust-generating, braking, and auto-rotation regimes. The auto-rotation range was clearly defined at the point where the current takes negative values.
  • Compressibility Effects: Applied dimensionless analysis, noting that the coefficients depend on the advance ratio, Reynolds number, and Mach number.
  • Efficiency Comparison: As illustrated in the final performance graph below, the optimal motor-propeller combination can be identified for each operating regime, allowing the specific influence of propeller diameter and pitch to be clearly observed.

Academic Projects at Politecnico di Milano

Selected MSc projects grouped by course, with each assignment problem presented as an individual portfolio entry.

Periodic Halo Orbits in the Earth–Moon CRTBP

Implemented the 3D Earth–Moon Circular Restricted Three-Body Problem (CRTBP) and computed the coordinates and Jacobi constants of the five Lagrange points with high-precision root finding.

Designed a differential correction scheme based on the State Transition Matrix to converge to periodic halo orbits with a prescribed Jacobi constant, and generated a family of solutions through numerical continuation.

Impulsive Earth–Moon Transfer Optimization in the PBRFBP

Designed a two-impulse Earth–Moon transfer in the Planar Bicircular Restricted Four-Body Problem (PBRFBP), starting from analytical parameters to generate a physically consistent first-guess trajectory and propagating it in both the Earth–Moon rotating frame and the Earth-centered inertial frame.

Formulated the transfer as a nonlinear constrained optimization problem, minimizing total \( \Delta V \) under dynamical and orbital boundary conditions. Implemented simple shooting without gradients, then enhanced the formulation by supplying analytical sensitivities via the State Transition Matrix, reducing iterations and improving convergence robustness.

Extended the approach to a multiple shooting scheme, enforcing continuity across several mesh points to better handle the strong nonlinearities of the PBRFBP and to explore lower-\( \Delta V \) solutions. Compared the resulting trajectories and cost with the single-shooting solutions, highlighting the benefits of mesh segmentation and gradient information.

Low-Thrust Debris-Aware Orbit Raising

Formulated a low-thrust orbit-raising maneuver from 800 km to 1000 km that minimizes collision risk by embedding a debris spatial density model into the performance index.

Applied Pontryagin’s Maximum Principle to derive state and costate dynamics, implemented the optimal thrust law, and used numerical continuation on maximum thrust to study how final time and propellant usage change.

Uncertainty Propagation Along an Earth–Moon Transfer

Propagated state uncertainty along an optimal Earth–Moon transfer in the planar bicircular restricted four-body problem using linear covariance propagation, the Unscented Transform, and Monte Carlo sampling.

Compared final 3σ position and velocity ellipses, time evolution of covariance, and QQ-plots to assess the validity of linear and Gaussian assumptions and the trade-off between accuracy and computational cost.

SMOS Orbit Determination with Batch Filters

Predicted visibility windows of the SMOS satellite from three ground stations, simulated azimuth, elevation, and range measurements with realistic noise, and solved a batch least-squares navigation problem.

Evaluated single-station versus multi-station configurations and the impact of including J2 dynamics on the estimated covariance for position, velocity, semimajor axis, and inclination, including a cost–accuracy trade-off on pass selection.

Lunar Lander Localization with Unscented Kalman Filters

Simulated a lunar orbiter and surface lander geometry, generated noisy orbiter position measurements and inter-satellite ranges, and implemented Unscented Kalman Filters to estimate the orbiter state.

Extended the state to include lander latitude and longitude and showed convergence of both orbiter state and lander coordinates by analyzing error evolution and 3σ bounds over the observation arc.
